Jog over to Joggins Fossil Cliffs

World Heritage Site

This 15 kilometer length of coast in Nova Scotia was designated a World Heritage site in 2008. On the banks of the Bay of Fundy, the most complete fossil record of the Coal Age is continuously being exposed by the highest tides in the world. Entombed in a fossil tree, early geologists found the first true reptile here.
